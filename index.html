Four delegations sat down on Mars this week to carve up gate routes nobody owned a decade ago. Three signed. The Jovian moons did not. A Ganymede attaché was heard to remark, on his way to the airlock, that you cannot divide a road that still kills the people who use it.

Mars is acting like the center of something. It can afford to. It has the lights, the relay, and most of the survivors who still have money. The rest of the system is invited to catch up, or to keep paying tolls.

Business InnovationSECTOR: MARKETS
Gina Motors Ships the First Mars-Built Mono-Wheel; Pippu Cola Buys the Side Panel
— Woolong Wire / Business Desk

Until this week, every vehicle on Mars arrived in a crate from somewhere else. On Tuesday a Tharsis assembly line — three years of borrowed tooling and stubbornness — rolled out its first homegrown mono-wheel, and Gina Motors became the first thing the frontier has built for itself instead of imported. It is ugly. It runs. Out here that is the whole review.

Pippu Cola bought the side panel before the paint dried. The drink is in every dome, every gate lounge, every relief crate; its founders understood early that on a planet with nothing, the company that sells you the small comfort wins. HexaChess, the puzzle outfit whose game has quietly eaten everyone's idle hours, says it'll license the dashboard next.

None of these names existed five years ago. That's the story of the section: in a system rebuilding from zero, the businesses being born now are the ones your grandchildren will think were always here.

Bell Peppers and Beef — the Official Special

Serves a hungry crew of three or four. The beef is aspirational. It usually is.
[ DROP YOUR PHOTO HERE — bell-peppers-and-beef.jpg ]
YOU'LL NEED
  • 4 green bell peppers, sliced thin
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar · a pinch of pepper
  • oil for the wok
  • beef, thin-sliced — if you caught a bounty this week
IF NO BEEF (THE USUAL CASE)
  • more bell peppers
  • a tin of whatever's in the locker
  • your dignity (optional)
  • the line "it's a lighter take"
  1. Get the wok smoking hot. This dish forgives nothing and rewards heat.
  2. If you have beef, sear it fast and set it aside. Try not to mention what it cost.
  3. Garlic and ginger into the hot oil — ten seconds, no more.
  4. Peppers in. Toss hard and keep them bright; limp peppers are a confession of defeat.
  5. Soy, oyster sauce, sugar, pepper. Return the beef, if it ever existed.
  6. Plate it like you meant it to be all peppers. Out here, conviction is half the meal.

Mini Guide — Growing Shiitake & Tomatoes in Space

For the ship cabin, the dome corner, or the maintenance bay nobody checks.

Shiitake. They don't want light, which makes them the easiest thing to grow in a place that hasn't any to spare.

[ DROP YOUR PHOTO HERE — shiitake.jpg ]
  1. Start with a hardwood block or sawdust brick already inoculated with shiitake spawn. Soak it 24 hours in clean water.
  2. Keep it cool and humid — a sealed crate with a damp cloth holds the air. Low light is fine; darkness is fine.
  3. In one to two weeks, pins appear. Mist daily. Don't drown them.
  4. Harvest by twisting, not cutting. Rest the block two weeks back in water and it fruits again. A good block feeds a crew for months.

Tomatoes. The opposite problem: greedy for light, and worth every watt.

[ DROP YOUR PHOTO HERE — tomatoes.jpg ]
  1. Pick a dwarf or "micro" variety — they fruit in small spaces and don't fight the ceiling.
  2. Grow in a bucket of inert medium fed with nutrient water (hydroponic is cleanest where soil is precious). Roots still need oxygen, so never leave them waterlogged.
  3. Give them the brightest light you've got, 14–16 hours a day. A cheap grow lamp beats a porthole.
  4. In zero-g there are no bees, so airflow does the pollinating — run a small fan, or flick each flower daily. No buzz, no fruit.
  5. Six to ten weeks to red. The first ripe one is traditionally eaten standing over the sink, alone, saying nothing.

About This Project
What This Is
— bokchoyCSS & Zi3600

The Weekly Woolong is a fan project set inside the Cowboy Bebop universe. It's an in-universe newspaper — the kind of thing that might actually exist in 2071, relayed across the solar system between bounty hunters, drifters, and whoever's left keeping the lights on.

Everything here follows the actual Watanabe/Cowboy Bebop canon. The lore, the timeline, the factions, the geography — we don't invent on top of it, we write inside it.

How the Articles Work
  • ▸ The IRSI — the Independent Ring of Satellite Intelligence — is our AI system. It's trained on Cowboy Bebop lore and reads source material before generating each article. It doesn't improvise; it works within the canon.
  • ▸ Articles update weekly, around the weekend. Each section — politics, economy, bounties, citizen stories — gets a fresh filing every issue.
  • ▸ Human editors set the rules, check the lore, and keep the voice consistent. The IRSI writes within those boundaries.
  • ▸ This is non-commercial. No ads, no paywall. Just a fan project we put work into because we think Cowboy Bebop deserves a proper living universe.

We hope you enjoy it as much as we do. Come back weekends for the new issue.
